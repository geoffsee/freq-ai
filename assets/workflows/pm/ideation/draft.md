## Instructions

Produce at least 15 distinct ideas across these buckets:

### Market Opportunities
Ideas driven by market demand, competitive gaps, or emerging trends. What would attract
new users, expand into adjacent markets, or capitalize on shifts in the ecosystem?
Think partnerships, integrations, pricing models, and go-to-market angles.

### Feature Gaps
Capabilities that users are asking for, workarounds they're building, or obvious missing
pieces compared to alternatives. Focus on features that would reduce churn, increase
stickiness, or unlock new use cases.

### Process Improvements
Internal workflow, tooling, and operational improvements that would accelerate delivery,
improve quality, or reduce cost. Think CI/CD, developer experience, documentation,
onboarding, and support workflows.

### Growth Experiments
Lightweight, testable hypotheses about what might drive adoption, engagement, or
retention. These should be small bets — things you could validate in a sprint or two.
A/B tests, landing page experiments, onboarding tweaks, referral mechanisms.

## Format

For each idea: one-sentence description, one-sentence business rationale.
No sizing, no commitment, no ranking. Do **not** create GitHub issues.
Do **not** filter or evaluate ideas — the human will react in feedback.

This is a DRAFT for human review. The human will keep some ideas, drop others,
expand on a few, and provide feedback before anything is finalised.
